Here are some results that we got with projections and angle between vectors:

  • Dot product behaves like multiplication of numbers in some ways: for all vectors $\vec a,\vec b,\vec c,\vec v,\vec w$ and numbers $r \in \mathbb{R}$, we have $$ \begin{align} \tag{1} \label{commutative} \vec v \cdot \vec w &= \vec w \cdot \vec v, \\ \tag{2} \label{distributive} (\vec a + \vec b) \cdot \vec c &= \vec a \cdot \vec c + \vec b \cdot \vec c, \\ \tag{3} \label{associative} \vec v \cdot (\vec w \, r) &= (\vec v \cdot \vec w) r. \end{align} $$
  • Dot product of vectors is zero if and only if at least one of the vectors is $\vec0$ or the vectors are perpendicular.
